About

I am an Interdisciplinary Engineering PhD candidate at Northeastern University focused on human-in-the-loop control and dynamic comfort modeling for civil infrastructure. My work connects building operations, machine learning, and behavioral insight so systems remain both efficient and accountable to people.

Previously, I worked as a Research Engineer at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL), where I contributed to large-scale building stock modeling, renewable pathway analysis, and software tools used by utility partners nationwide.

Experience Highlights

  • Northeastern University (PhD): Research on dynamic comfort and human-building interaction in grid-interactive efficient buildings.
  • NREL (Research Engineer): Improved U.S. housing stock modeling coverage and reduced simulation cost through cloud/HPC Python workflows.
  • Applied ML & Energy Systems: Reinforcement learning and optimization approaches for distributed energy and building operations.
